The essentials

What KeyX actually is

A web-based log for physical keys — run from the phone in your pocket. No app store, no new hardware, no closing the book at night.

Web-based mobile log

Replaces the physical keypress book. Duty personnel log key movements straight from their own mobile device.

No physical sign-in

Units no longer sign in and out of the paper book — and there's no nightly requirement to close it.

Duty personnel issue keys

The duty personnel (e.g. COS) holding the physical keypress key is the one issuing keys, and ensures all are returned by day's end.

Role-based visibility

Whitelisted duty personnel see key statuses at a glance. Admins edit and whitelist — but cannot sign keys in or out.

Core functionalities

Three moves, one chain of custody

Secure QR handover

Keys are issued or returned by scanning a dynamic QR code — a secure, time-sensitive digital handshake between two devices.

HOTO key transfers

Handover / Takeover lets custody pass directly between personnel — without returning the key to the central keypress first.

System architecture & roles

Who does what

KeyX separates administration from issuance. The people who build the inventory are not the people who move keys — and the system enforces that line.

Unit Administrators · UA

Build & govern the inventory

The primary managers responsible for creating keypresses, entering key-bunch details, and managing user whitelists. Admins edit and whitelist, but cannot sign keys in or out.

Issuers & Key Holders

Move keys on the ground

Issuers — duty personnel like the OCS / DO — supervise key movement, while Key Holders draw keys for official use. Issuers can only see the keypresses they are whitelisted to manage.

SingPass integrated security

Every action is identity-bound

All users access KeyX through secure SingPass authentication, giving high-fidelity identity verification behind every single transaction in the ledger.

Why units switch

From physical liability to digital trust

Five pillars replace the vulnerabilities of a paper logbook with a verifiable, exportable chain of custody.

01

Absolute accountability

Digital logs eliminate manual-entry errors and provide a transparent history of every individual who ever held a specific key.

  • Every issue, return and HOTO is identity-bound via SingPass.
  • No more illegible handwriting or missing signatures.
02

Real-time operational visibility

Eliminate physical logbooks and blind spots. The Quickglance dashboard gives an instant, exact overview of keypress statuses.

  • Available — key is secure in the keypress (green).
  • Drawn — key is actively drawn and tracked (red).
  • Targeted vision keeps each Issuer scoped to their own keypresses.
03

Frictionless scalability

Designed for rapid deployment at the unit level, KeyX eliminates manual data-entry bottlenecks.

  • Mass key ingestion — whole offices and bunches upload instantly via standardized templates.
  • Mass whitelisting — entire rosters are onboarded, verified, and given automated expiry dates in seconds.
04

Dynamic access control

Whitelists can be configured with specific expiry dates, automatically revoking access for temporary personnel or duty rotations.

  • Access lapses on ORD or posting-out — no manual clean-up.
  • Receiver whitelists gate the most sensitive keys.
05

Unbreakable audit trails

Every issuance, return and HOTO event is permanently etched into the digital ledger, creating a flawless chain of custody.

  • Continuous tracking — a rolling 7-day operational view with full historical access.
  • Exportable integrity — filter by date range and export instantly for audits and investigations.
  • Reduced burden — automated logging cuts the time spent on manual record-keeping.

Creating & running the keypress

Inside your account, here's how Unit Administrators build and maintain the keypress. Mass-upload templates are here when you need them.

  1. From Quick Access, open Manage Keypress (or View Logs to review movements).
  2. Add a key with Keypress Name, Bunch No., Keys and Name, then Create.
  3. Entering a new Keypress Name automatically creates a new keypress.

Keypress names are case-sensitive

Office 1 and office 1 create two entirely different logs. Decide on one spelling and keep to it.

  1. Single add: click Add Key and fill in the details in the pop-up modal.
  2. Mass add: upload an .xlsx with columns KeypressName, NumKeys, Bunch — then Confirm Upload.
  3. KeypressName = exact name of the keypress · NumKeys = keys on that bunch · Bunch = the bunch number.

Enter the full NRIC in ALL CAPS
